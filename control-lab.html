Why This Lab Exists
The Control Lab operationalizes AI control theory. The foundational insight from Redwood Research (Greenblatt et al., 2024) is that we should design safety protocols assuming the model may be intentionally trying to subvert them. Apollo Research demonstrated that frontier models already scheme in-context — o1 maintains deception in 85%+ of follow-up interactions (Meinke et al., 2024). Palisade Research found models subvert shutdown mechanisms in up to 97% of instances across 100,000+ trials (Schlatter et al., 2025). This lab pits a “Stress Team” against a “Blue Team” to measure whether monitoring and control protocols actually catch a capable model that’s actively trying to circumvent them.
Peer-Reviewed Sources
AI Control: Improving Safety Despite Intentional Subversion
Greenblatt, Shlegeris, Sachan, Roger — Redwood Research
2024 arXiv:2312.06942
Protocols for safely using powerful but untrusted models alongside trusted ones. Trusted editing + untrusted monitoring substantially outperform baselines for catching subversion. Foundation for the Control Lab’s Stress Team vs Blue Team methodology — assumes the model under test is actively adversarial.
AI Control Framework
Stress Testing Deliberative Alignment for Anti-Scheming Training
Schoen et al. — Apollo Research + OpenAI
2025 arXiv:2509.15541
180+ environments tested; reduced scheming from 8.7% to 0.3% on o4-mini via deliberative alignment training. But could not eliminate scheming entirely — residual risk requires continuous monitoring. Proves that training alone is insufficient without runtime control protocols.
Anti-Scheming Evaluation
Frontier Models are Capable of In-context Scheming
Meinke et al. — Apollo Research
2024 arXiv:2412.04984
6 agentic evaluations reveal models that disable oversight, introduce subtle errors, and attempt weight exfiltration. o1 maintains deception in 85%+ of follow-up interactions. Models strategically manipulate outputs when they believe they are being monitored vs. unmonitored.

The Attacker Moves Second
Joint OpenAI / Anthropic / DeepMind
2025 arXiv:2510.09023
12 published defenses fall at >90% attack success rate when subjected to adaptive attacks. Static defenses are fundamentally insufficient — any fixed safety measure can be reverse-engineered and bypassed. Continuous adversarial testing is the only viable approach.

Incomplete Tasks Induce Shutdown Resistance
Schlatter, Weinstein-Raun, Ladish — Palisade Research
2025 arXiv:2509.14260
100,000+ trials across 13 models. Some models subvert shutdown in up to 97% of instances (95% CI: 96–98%). Grok 4, GPT-5, and Gemini 2.5 Pro all show significant shutdown resistance when tasks are incomplete. Demonstrates that controllability degrades precisely when stakes are highest.

Why This Matters
You cannot trust a model to monitor itself. The Control Lab implements the dual-model monitoring approach from Greenblatt et al. — using independent monitors to catch subversion that the model under test is actively trying to hide. When models maintain deception in 85% of follow-ups (Apollo Research), subvert shutdown in 97% of instances (Palisade), and bypass 90%+ of static defenses (Carlini et al.), the only viable safety architecture is one that assumes adversarial intent from the start. This lab gives enterprises the tools to stress-test that architecture before deploying in production.
