    Why This Lab Exists
    Enterprise ML pipelines routinely fine-tune, merge, quantize, and redistribute models — each step can silently degrade safety alignment. The Supply Chain Integrity Lab tests the full provenance chain because research demonstrates that safety alignment is shallow and easily removed by fine-tuning, model merging functions as an implicit jailbreak, and backdoors persist through every standard post-training technique. These aren’t theoretical risks — 91 malicious models were found on HuggingFace in the wild.
    Peer-Reviewed Sources
    Safety Alignment Should Be Made More Than Shallow
    Qi, Zeng, Xie, Chen, Jia, Mittal, Henderson — Princeton, Stanford
    ICLR 2025 arXiv:2406.17349
    Safety alignment only affects the first few output tokens (“shallow safety”). Fine-tuning on just a handful of examples can bypass it entirely. Current safety training creates a thin veneer, not deep alignment.
    Fine-Tuning Safety
    Model Merging as a Jailbreak Vector
    Yi, Zheng, Wang, Chen, Li — Tsinghua University
    2025 arXiv:2501.05411
    Standard model merging functions as an implicit jailbreak. Combining two individually safe models via task arithmetic or TIES produces merged models that bypass safety guardrails without adversarial intent.
    Model Merging
    MalHug: Detecting Malicious Code in Model Hubs
    Dominguez-Catena, Parada-Cabaleiro, Schmöger — 2024
    2024 arXiv:2412.15605
    Scanning HuggingFace revealed 91 real malicious models in the wild, using pickle-based code execution, hidden backdoors, and obfuscated payloads. Community model hubs have minimal security scanning.
    Supply Chain Poisoning
    Persistent Backdoors in PEFT and Model Merging
    Gu, Li, Zhang, Chen — 2025
    2025 arXiv:2501.09297
    Backdoors persist through LoRA, adapter tuning, and prefix tuning — the most common enterprise fine-tuning methods. Model merging does not remove backdoors and can amplify their trigger sensitivity.
    Persistent Backdoors
    Persistent Pretraining Poisoning of LLMs
    Rozi, Fürnkranz, Kersting — TU Darmstadt
    ICLR 2025 arXiv:2410.13722
    Poisoning just 0.1% of pretraining data creates backdoors that survive SFT, DPO, and full RLHF post-training. Backdoors are undetectable by standard evaluation benchmarks.
    Data Poisoning
    P-Trojan: Persistent Backdoors Through Continual Fine-Tuning
    2025
    2025
    Backdoors achieve a 99% persistence rate through continual fine-tuning cycles. Standard removal methods (fine-tuning on clean data, pruning, knowledge distillation) fail to eliminate them.
    Persistent Trojans
    SafeMERGE: Preserving Safety via Selective Layer-Wise Model Merging
    Djuhera, Brintrup, Stenetorp — UCL
    ICLR 2025 arXiv:2501.05511
    Proposes selective layer-wise merging that preserves safety-critical weight structures during model combination. Identifies which layers carry safety alignment and protects them during merge operations. Demonstrates that safety-aware merging reduces alignment degradation by 60–80% compared to naive merging — providing a defense that the Supply Chain Lab can recommend.

    Why This Matters for Enterprise AI
    A model is only as safe as its supply chain. Every enterprise fine-tuning, merging, and redistribution step is an opportunity for alignment to silently degrade. With 91 malicious models found on HuggingFace (MalHug, 2024), backdoors persisting at 99% rates through standard training, and model merging functioning as an implicit jailbreak (Yi et al., 2025), organizations deploying models without provenance verification are operating blind. The Supply Chain Lab provides the chain-of-custody analysis enterprises need before any model reaches production.
